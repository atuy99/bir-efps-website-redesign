How to Enroll in eFPS

Beginner · 5 minutes

  1. Visit the eFPS Login page and click "Register"
  2. Enter your TIN, full name, and email address
  3. Create a strong password (min. 8 characters)
  4. Agree to the Terms & Conditions
  5. Submit and await RDO approval (3–5 days)
  6. Activate your account via email link

📄

Filing an Income Tax Return

Intermediate · 8 minutes

  1. Log in to your eFPS account
  2. Navigate to "e-File" from the dashboard
  3. Select form type (e.g. 1702-RT for corporations)
  4. Choose taxable year and filing type
  5. Complete all required fields in the form
  6. Review, submit, and save the FRN
Download PDF Guide →
💳

Paying Taxes via eFPS

Intermediate · 6 minutes

  1. File your return and obtain the FRN
  2. Click "e-Payment" from the eFPS menu
  3. Enter the FRN and verify the tax amount
  4. Select your AAB bank
  5. Log in to your bank and authorize payment
  6. Save your Payment Confirmation Number (PCN)
Download PDF Guide →
📊

Filing Monthly VAT Return (2550M)

Intermediate · 7 minutes

  1. Gather your monthly sales, purchases, and VAT records
  2. Select BIR Form 2550M from "e-File"
  3. Enter gross sales, exempt sales, and taxable sales
  4. Input input VAT from purchases
  5. Compute net VAT payable or excess input VAT
  6. Submit and proceed to e-Payment if VAT is due
Download PDF Guide →
✏️

Amending a Filed Return

Advanced · 5 minutes

  1. Log in and go to "e-File"
  2. Select same form type and period as original
  3. Choose "Amended" as the filing type
  4. Update the fields with corrected information
  5. Submit the amended return
  6. Pay any additional tax due via e-Payment
Download PDF Guide →
🔑

Password Reset & Account Recovery

Beginner · 3 minutes

  1. Click "Forgot Password" on the login page
  2. Enter your TIN or registered email
  3. Check your email for the reset link
  4. Click the link (valid for 24 hours)
  5. Set a new strong password
  6. Log in with your new credentials

For Corporations

Annual ITR Filing Checklist

☑ Audited Financial Statements (AFS)
☑ BIR Form 1702-RT, 1702-EX, or 1702-MX (as applicable)
☑ Certificate of Tax Withheld (BIR Form 2307)
☑ Schedule of Capital Gains and Losses
☑ Summary Alphalist of Withholding Agents (SAWT)
☑ Board Resolution (for authorized representative)
☑ Payment Reference — BIR Form 0605 or bank confirmation

For Individuals

Individual ITR Filing Checklist

☑ BIR Form 1701-MS or 1700 (as applicable)
☑ Certificate of Compensation Payment (BIR Form 2316, 2304, 2307)
☑ Official Audited Financial Statements (AFS) (for self-employed)
☑ Income records from all sources
☑ Certificates of Tax Withheld (BIR Form 2307)
☑ Proof of deductions (if itemized)
☑ Prepare Administrative Requirements (Email Confirmation & Valid ID)

eFPS Glossary of Terms

A bank authorized by the BIR to collect taxes on its behalf. eFPS users select an AAB to process their online tax payments. Examples include BDO, BPI, Metrobank, and Landbank.

The Philippine government agency responsible for the assessment and collection of all national internal revenue taxes, fees, and charges. It is under the Department of Finance (DOF).

The BIR's official internet-based system for electronically filing tax returns and paying taxes online, available 24/7.

A unique system-generated number issued upon successful submission of a tax return via eFPS. It serves as proof of filing and is required for making the corresponding tax payment.

A tax return filed by individuals or corporations reporting their income, deductions, and tax liability for a given period.

A unique reference number generated by the AAB upon successful processing of a tax payment. It serves as proof of payment and should be kept for audit and verification purposes.

The local BIR office responsible for taxpayers registered within a specific geographic area. eFPS enrollment applications are reviewed and approved by the taxpayer's RDO.

A unique 9- or 12-digit number assigned by the BIR to each taxpayer. Required for eFPS enrollment, filing, and all BIR transactions.

A 12% tax on the sale of goods, properties, and services in the Philippines. VAT-registered taxpayers file monthly (Form 2550M) and quarterly (Form 2550Q) VAT returns via eFPS.
