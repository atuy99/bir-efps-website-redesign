General Information

The Electronic Filing and Payment System (eFPS) is the Bureau of Internal Revenue's official internet-based platform that allows taxpayers to electronically file their tax returns and pay their tax liabilities online.

eFPS is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, providing convenience and efficiency for taxpayers across the Philippines and abroad.

The following taxpayers are mandated to file and pay via eFPS:

  • Top 5,000 private corporations (as identified by the BIR)
  • Insurance companies and stock brokers
  • Large taxpayers enrolled under the Large Taxpayers Service
  • Government-owned and controlled corporations (GOCCs)
  • Taxpayers identified under specific Revenue Memorandum Orders

Other taxpayers may voluntarily enroll in eFPS.

eFPS supports electronic filing of the following forms (among others):

  • BIR Form 1700/1701/1702 — Annual Income Tax Returns
  • BIR Form 2550M/2550Q — Monthly/Quarterly VAT Returns
  • BIR Form 1601-C — Monthly Withholding Tax (Compensation)
  • BIR Form 1601-EQ/FQ — Expanded Withholding Tax Returns
  • BIR Form 2551Q — Quarterly Percentage Tax
  • BIR Form 2000/2000-OT — Documentary Stamp Tax
  • BIR Form 1800/1801 — Estate and Donor's Tax Returns

No. There is no filing fee for using the eFPS. The system is provided free of charge to all enrolled taxpayers. However, your bank may impose transaction fees for online tax payments processed through their platform.

  • Convenience: File from anywhere with internet access, 24/7.

Enrollment

To enroll in eFPS, follow these steps:

  • Click "Register" on the eFPS login page.
  • Enter your valid TIN, full name, email address, and create a password.
  • Submit the application form.
  • Wait for approval from your Revenue District Office (RDO). Approval typically takes 3–5 business days.
  • Upon approval, you will receive an email notification with your access credentials.
  • Valid and active Tax Identification Number (TIN)
  • Complete and updated registration with the BIR (BIR Form 1901/1903)
  • Active email address for system notifications
  • Enrollment in at least one BIR-accredited Authorized Agent Bank (AAB) for payment

eFPS enrollment typically takes 3 to 5 business days from submission. The RDO reviews your application and either approves or requests additional information. You will be notified via email upon decision.

For urgent enrollment, contact your RDO directly. Large Taxpayer Service (LTS) enrollments may be processed faster.

Yes. Under the Ease of Paying Taxes Act (EOPT) and recent BIR circulars, the online application process allows fully remote enrollment. You submit your application through the eFPS portal and your RDO reviews it electronically. Physical visits are generally not required unless the RDO requests supporting documents.

Tax Filing

  • Log in to your eFPS account.
  • Select "e-File" from the main menu.
  • Choose the tax form type and the applicable period.
  • Fill in the required fields or upload the completed form.
  • Review all entries and click "Submit".
  • You will receive a Filing Reference Number (FRN) as proof of submission.

The Filing Reference Number (FRN) is a unique system-generated number that confirms your tax return has been successfully received by the BIR. It serves as your official proof of filing and should be kept for your records.

The FRN is also required when making the corresponding tax payment through the eFPS e-Payment facility.

Yes. You can file an amended return through eFPS by selecting "Amended Return" as the filing type. The amendment must be filed within three (3) years from the original filing date to avoid penalties.

Note that if the amendment results in additional tax due, interest and penalties may apply on the deficiency amount. Voluntary amendments before BIR assessment generally result in lower penalties.

Yes. eFPS filers are categorized into groups (Group A–E) based on industry, and each group is assigned a staggered filing deadline — typically extending 5 days beyond the standard deadline. Consult the BIR Tax Calendar for your specific group and deadline.

Category

Payment

  • After filing, click "e-Payment" from the eFPS dashboard.
  • Enter your Filing Reference Number (FRN).
  • Select your AAB (Authorized Agent Bank).
  • Enter payment details (amount, account number).
  • Confirm the payment — you will be redirected to your bank's payment portal.
  • Upon successful payment, you receive a Payment Confirmation Number (PCN).

The following banks are accredited as AABs for eFPS payments: BDO Unibank, BPI, Metrobank, UnionBank, Landbank, Development Bank of the Philippines (DBP), Philippine National Bank (PNB), RCBC, China Banking Corporation, Security Bank, EastWest Bank, and others.

You must have an existing account with the chosen AAB. Contact your bank to enroll your account for eFPS payment transactions.

If your payment fails or you do not receive a Payment Confirmation Number (PCN), follow these steps:

  • Check your email for any bank confirmation or error message.
  • Log in to eFPS and review your payment status under "Payment History".
  • Contact your bank to verify if the transaction was debited from your account.
  • If debited but no PCN received, contact the eFPS helpdesk (8759-5400) with your bank transaction reference.

Technical Issues

Click "Forgot Password" on the eFPS login page. Enter your registered email address or TIN and follow the instructions sent to your email. If your email address has changed, contact your RDO for manual password reset assistance.

eFPS supports the latest versions of the following browsers:

  • Google Chrome (recommended)
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Apple Safari

Ensure JavaScript is enabled and pop-up blockers are disabled for the eFPS domain. Clear your browser cache if you experience display issues.

eFPS sessions expire after 20 minutes of inactivity for security purposes. To avoid losing your work:

  • Prepare all data before starting the filing process.
  • Save your work frequently using the "Save Draft" function if available.
  • Ensure you have a stable internet connection.
  • Avoid opening multiple eFPS tabs in the same browser.

Contact the BIR eFPS Helpdesk through any of these channels:

  • 📞 Phone: +63 2 8759-5400 (available 24/7)
  • ✉ Email: contact_us@bir.gov.ph
  • Provide your TIN, the error message or screenshot, and the time the error occurred.

Security & Privacy

Yes. eFPS uses TLS 1.3 encryption to protect all data transmitted between your browser and the BIR servers. Payment transactions are processed through accredited banks with their own security infrastructure. The system complies with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173).

No. The BIR will NEVER ask for your password, OTP, or full TIN via email or text message. If you receive such communication, do NOT click any links or provide any information. Report phishing attempts to contact_us@bir.gov.ph and change your eFPS password immediately.

  • Use a strong, unique password (at least 8 characters with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols).
  • Never share your password with anyone, including BIR staff.
  • Log out completely after every session.
  • Do not access eFPS on public or shared computers.
  • Change your password regularly (every 90 days recommended).
  • Verify you are on the official eFPS site before entering credentials.
